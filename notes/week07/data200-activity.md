# Data Systems: Real-World Reflections

DATA 400: Capstone in Data Analytics

75 min

# What are we doing today?

Today we are doing small-group work instead of going around the table one by one.

You will share your 1-2 things in small groups, pick one example together, put a short headline on the board, and present it to the class.

Your example could be something that worked well, something that surprised you, or something that went wrong. Anything that taught you how data systems work in practice.

# Step 1: Share in your group (5 min)

Each person shares 1-2 things from an internship, research job, campus job, capstone, or any data-related work.

# Step 2: Pick one example (8 min)

As a group, choose the example with the clearest connection to DATA 200: Data Systems for Data Analytics.

Shape your example:

| | |
|---------|--------|
| **Context** | What were you doing? Where did the data live? (files, SQL, cloud, etc.) |
| **What happened** | What did you notice or learn? (one sentence) |
| **Why it mattered** | Why was this important or interesting? |
| **DATA 200 connection** | What concept showed up here, and what would you tell a classmate about it? |

# What counts as a tie-in?

Examples: schema, keys, joins, pipelines, data wrangling, naming conventions, permissions, environments, versioning, reproducibility, data quality checks, documentation, source of truth, webscraping.

# Step 3: Write on the board (5 min)

One person from your group writes a short title (3-6 words).

Put it under one of these headings: Pipelines / Automation, Schemas / Keys / Joins, Cleaning / Data Quality, Access / Permissions, Versioning / Reproducibility, Source of Truth / Documentation, Webscraping, Other

# Step 4: Present (about 5 min per group)

Walk through your example using the outline.

# Step 5: Wrap-up discussion (15 min)

Using the board: Which topics showed up the most? What patterns do we see? What should you build into your capstone workflow now?

# Exit note (5 min)

Write one sentence on the board before you leave:

"One thing from today I will think about when working with data in the future is: ________________"

# Good luck!
